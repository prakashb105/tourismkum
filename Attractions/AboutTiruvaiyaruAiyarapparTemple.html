The Aiyarappar Temple, also known as the Tiruvaiyaru Aiyarappar Temple, is a prominent Hindu temple in the town of Tiruvaiyaru near Kumbakonam in Tamil Nadu, India. This temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, worshipped here as "Aiyarappar" (meaning "The Noble Lord"). It stands as a historical and architectural gem, revered by both devotees and historians.

Believed to have been built during the Chola period, the temple has seen renovations and additions over time by rulers such as the Nayaks and Marathas. Tiruvaiyaru is also the birthplace of Saint Thyagaraja, the celebrated Carnatic music composer, honored annually in the Thyagaraja Aradhana festival nearby.

The temple’s architecture is a stunning example of Dravidian style, with towering gopurams (entrance towers), intricately carved pillars, and a vast complex. Its five prakarams (circumambulatory paths) symbolically represent the five elements, highlighting the temple’s cosmic design.

Lord Shiva, in the form of Aiyarappar, is the main deity here, accompanied by Goddess Dharmasamvardhini. The temple is also known for the Saptasthanam festival, celebrating a symbolic pilgrimage where the temple’s deity visits seven sacred temples in the area, symbolizing the unity of the region’s spiritual practices.

Situated on the banks of the sacred Kaveri River, the Aiyarappar Temple holds special significance, adding to its reverence. The temple also features a shrine for Saint Appar (Tirunavukkarasar), a revered Saivite saint who composed several hymns here in praise of Lord Shiva. Celebrated for its historical roots, artistic beauty, and spiritual importance, the Aiyarappar Temple is a cultural and architectural heritage site, embodying the religious and artistic spirit of Tamil Nadu.
